The Standard That Separates V3 From Every Other Indian Merchant Exporter

Most Indian merchant exporters are commercial traders. They source a product, collect whatever documents the manufacturer provides, and ship. Whether those documents meet your importing authority's format, content, or validity requirements — that is your problem.

V3 operates differently. Regulatory documentation is prepared and verified in-house by Vicharvanta Nabar — a practitioner with 15 years of pharmaceutical regulatory affairs experience. Not an outsourced consultant. Not a compliance officer reviewing a checklist. A career RA specialist who has submitted CTD/ACTD dossiers, handled MOH queries, reviewed country-specific label artwork, and managed regulatory portfolios across Latin America, CIS, and Southeast Asia simultaneously.

This capability sits inside V3's founding structure. It is not a service you pay for separately. It is how every export at V3 is handled.

The result: your documentation is built for your market before your order ships. Gaps are identified here, not at your customs authority.

Every export documentation package — CTD/ACTD dossiers, COPP, Free Sale Certificates, artwork — is reviewed in-house against the specific requirements of your importing authority. Documentation failures at customs are not a V3 problem. They are what V3 was built to prevent.

02 — Supply Network
WHO-GMP Certified Manufacturers — Verified, Not Listed

Every manufacturing partner in V3's network entered through direct professional relationships built over 15 years in pharmaceutical regulatory affairs — not sourced from export directories or trade databases. Each facility holds current WHO-GMP certification. When you request documentation proof, it exists. Where multiple certified manufacturers can produce your product, V3 sources across them — so supply continuity is structural, not dependent on a single facility.

03 — Sourcing Model
We Source What You Need, Not What We Have in Stock

V3 does not publish a product catalogue. 04 — Direct Access

How We Source and Export Pharmaceuticals

Submit Your Product Requirement

Send us the product name, quantity, and destination country. No catalogue browsing, no back-and-forth about what's in stock — V3 works inquiry-driven. Your requirement drives the sourcing. We confirm receipt and respond with a timeline within one business day.

Manufacturer Sourcing

We match your requirement to our network of WHO-GMP certified manufacturers — verified partners sourced through our in-house regulatory team's professional relationships, not through directories. Where one manufacturer covers the product, you get dedicated supply. Where multiple manufacturers can produce it, you get competitive pricing options and supply continuity built in.

Quotation and Documentation Package

You receive a complete quotation: product specifications, unit pricing, MOQ, lead time, and an outline of the export documentation included with your shipment. Pricing is transparent. Nothing is held back for later negotiation.

Quality Verification

Before the order is confirmed, we verify manufacturer compliance documentation — WHO-GMP certificates, Certificate of Pharmaceutical Product (COPP), and facility audit status. Our in-house Regulatory Affairs specialist reviews all documentation against the requirements of your importing country. You receive confirmation before production begins.

Regulatory Packaging and Market Compliance

Labelling, artwork, and packaging are prepared to meet the regulatory requirements of your market — whether that is INVIMA (Colombia), DIGEMID (Peru), MOH standards in the Middle East, or NAFDAC (Nigeria). Our regulatory team handles country-specific artwork review and pre-shipment documentation as part of the standard export process, not as an add-on.

Shipment and Delivery Coordination

Once the order is cleared for dispatch, we coordinate shipping, arrange the full pre-shipment documentation package (Certificate of Origin, Commercial Invoice, Packing List, COA, COPP, and applicable Free Sale Certificate), and keep you informed through to delivery confirmation. Your shipment arrives with documentation your customs authority will accept.
